## `{ philosophy }` &nbsp; How I Think

> *"A developer who only knows one layer of the stack is at the mercy of every other layer."*

Three things I care about deeply:

**→ Architecture before code**
Before writing a single line, I want to understand the system. Where does data live? How does it move? What breaks under load? These questions shape everything downstream.

**→ Cross-domain fluency**
Most developers specialize. That's fine. I've chosen to go wide because understanding how a JVM application works makes me a better Node.js developer, and vice versa. Every language teaches you something the others can't.

**→ Responsibility in leadership**
Being a co-founder at 18 means owning decisions — including the ones that are wrong. I take that seriously. When something breaks, I don't look for who's to blame. I fix it.
